SmiteForge: A Companion App

Purpose: Design a Smite 2 companion tool that gives players quick, organized access to gods, abilities, builds, and match stats without unnecessary clutter.

Objective:Create two separate native mobile apps — one for iOS and one for Android — that feel fully at home on their respective platforms while maintaining a consistent brand identity and core feature set.

Role: UX/UI Designer (sole designer on project)

Duration: 5 weeks (from research to platform-specific high-fidelity prototype)

View the Full Case Study

Research

I began by researching how Smite players access build and stat data, reviewing community tools, forums, and player feedback.

Ideation

The insights were clear: players wanted a fast, reliable, and visually cohesive tool that didn't sacrifice usability for aesthetics.

Wireframe

I moved to digital wireframes early to align with each platform's design guidelines. I used SF Pro, tab-based navigation, and fluid transitions for iOS, and Roboto, elevation cues, and platform-specific navigation for Android.

Android Mid Fidelity Screens

Both versions used Smite-inspired visuals, iconography, and a dark theme with electric blue and gold accents to mirror the game’s layout, helping players feel instantly familiar.
